Frog Cat

Course: CDM 175 Digital Game Design

"Frog Cat" was developed over the course of two weeks. Our aim was to create a piece of electronic literature, digital poetry, interactive fiction, or a choose-your-own-adventure experience using a text-based interface. Designed with Twine, "Frog Cat" draws inspiration from classic children's books like "If You Give a Mouse a Cookie" and "Are You My Mother?". In this interactive story, you guide a little black cat on his journey to make new friends. The choices you make along the way will lead to different outcomes—will you be able to help Frog Cat find companionship?

Fruit Ninja

Course: CDM 175 Digital Game Design

This abstract arcade game challenges players to collect 100 fruits—think you can do it? The concept is straightforward, but engaging. For this project, my primary focus was on designing the UI and scripting the randomization of falling fruits and hazardous objects. My goal was to create a visually appealing user interface that tracks progress while making the gameplay feel "juicy" and satisfying.

Escape from School

Course: CDM 175 Digital Game Design

"Escape from School" was the final project of the course. I worked on it with a group of two other people. I worked on putting together the sprites, developing the scenes, drawing the main character, and writing the script. We aimed to tell the story of a young boy trapped in a school bathroom, desperately searching for a way to escape—if one exists. As the story unfolds, players will need to pay close attention to even the smallest details. The game may be short, but there's more to the story than meets the eye—can you piece it all together?

Battle of the... Birds?

Course: DES 157A Interactive Media II

This studio project provided an opportunity to delve deeper into UX design while strengthening our skills in HTML, CSS, and especially JavaScript. What started as a simple goal of designing a Pig Game evolved into a more strategic 1v1 fighter game, using the same mechanics of players taking turns. To add a personal touch, I gave users the ability to choose their own adorable bird mascot. I hope you enjoy the experience!

25 Days as a College Student

Course: DES 157B Interactive Media III

"25 Days as a College Student" is the culmination of a semester-long capstone project, centered around the theme "Designing Your Future." While topics like technology, health, and climate change were on the table, I chose to focus on education. I created a sim-like interactive project that compels users to make tough choices, each leading to different outcomes. My goal is to help users realize just how challenging higher education can be and to encourage them to brainstorm ways that education can improve in the future. The project required us to: Develop and design compelling interactive narratives for our content and data. Design and implement opportunities for the audience to engage with and take action on the project. Create a feedback loop that reinforces the goals, narrative, and data of the project.
